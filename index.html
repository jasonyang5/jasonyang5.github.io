Solow Growth Model and Data

A Web Application for viewing data relevant to the Solow Growth Model

Motivation and Basic Theory

The Solow Growth model predicts the growth rate and steady state of a country based off of 3 main things:

Production Technology
Takes Labor and Capital to produce output (GDP)
Savings Rate
Amount of capital from last year reinvested into capital for this year
Labor vs Capital
Amount of Capital relative to Labor
Steady State
The state when capital last year equals capital this year (and similarly for production)

Based off of this, the Solow model has 2 main predictions:

  • Poorer countries grow faster than wealthier countries
  • Countries with the same growth statistics (from above) should eventually converge to the same level of consumption (i.e. resources available) at steady state

    • For more advanced treatment, see an intermediate macroeconomics textbook
